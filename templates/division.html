Dividing Binary Numbers

Binary division follows the same logic as long division in decimal. The steps involve checking how many times the divisor fits into the dividend — only now, you're working with just 1 and 0. Borrowing and subtraction still happen, just like in base-10.

Binary Division Rules

Before we begin, remember these basic operations:

  • 0 ÷ 1 = 0
  • 1 ÷ 1 = 1
  • 1 ÷ 0 → undefined (just like in decimal)
  • Division is a repeated subtraction process

Example: Divide 110100 by 110

We'll walk through dividing 110100 (which is 52 in decimal) by 110 (which is 6 in decimal).

  1. Start with the leftmost digits of the dividend until you have a number equal or greater than the divisor.
  2. Divide that portion, subtract the divisor, and bring down the next bit.
  3. Repeat the process until no more bits remain.

The full process mimics the way you’d do decimal long division — just with 0s and 1s. At each step, you check if the current portion is large enough to subtract the divisor.

Final Answer

The quotient of 110100 ÷ 110 is 111 (which is 7 in decimal), and the remainder is 0. You can verify this by multiplying: 111 × 110 = 110100.

How to Check Your Answer

To verify binary division, convert both the dividend and divisor to decimal. Perform normal division and convert the result back to binary. This is a great way to double-check your work!
