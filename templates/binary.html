Understanding Binary Numbers

Binary numbers — just 1s and 0s — might seem confusing at first, but they're actually very straightforward once you see how they work. Computers love binary because everything can be broken down into two states: ON or OFF, True or False, 1 or 0.

The binary system is called base-2. Each digit (called a bit) represents a power of 2, moving from right to left:

... 32 | 16 | 8 | 4 | 2 | 1

Example: Convert 30 to Binary

We subtract powers of 2:

  • 30 - 16 → 14 → add a 1
  • 14 - 8 → 6 → add a 1
  • 6 - 4 → 2 → add a 1
  • 2 - 2 → 0 → add a 1
  • No 1s left → add a 0

Result: 11110

Another Example: Convert 41 to Binary

Following the same logic:

  • 41 - 32 → 9 → 1
  • 9 - 8 → 1 → 1
  • Remaining 1 → ends with a 1

Result: 101001
